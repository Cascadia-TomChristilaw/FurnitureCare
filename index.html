Wood Care - Wipe Down and Inspection

This is the most important step. Fill a bucket with a gallon or two of piping hot water. You can add a capful of Murphy's oil soap if you like. This will help with heavily soiled pieces, and has a pleasant , clean smell.

When the water has cooled enough to put your hands in, soak a terry cloth, or other clean rag in the water and wring it VERY well, so that it is barely damp. The moisture should evaporate behind the cloth as you wipe. The object is to leave no standing water on the surface.

Starting at the top of the piece, wipe each surface clean with your prepared cloth, and carefully inspect each area for defects, scratches, etc. These can be marked with bits of painter's tape to make them easier to find after wipe down.
Work your way over the piece from top to bottom until you have wiped and inspected each surface, and identified any problems needing attention.

At this point, you can proceed to repair, or if no none are needed, the piece is ready to be polished if desired, or returned to use.

What is "Deluxing"?

Deluxing is the art of restoring a piece of furniture to like-new, pristine condition.

Deluxe Like A Pro will provide insightful information on how to maintain your furniture and keep it in its best cosmetic and structural condition.
